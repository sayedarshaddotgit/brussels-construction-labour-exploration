# Exploring Migrant Labour in Brussels' Construction Sector

An exploratory study using Belgian Census 2011 and GIS data to investigate the composition of the construction workforce in Brussels.

## Motivation

This project was undertaken to familiarize myself with Belgian census and spatial datasets relevant to research on labour, migration, and urban development. The objective was not to produce a complete research study, but to explore available data sources, understand their structure, and assess their potential for studying labour dynamics in the construction sector.

## Data Sources

- Belgian Census 2011 (Statbel)
- Census Hypercube HC22
- Census Hypercube HC43
- Belgian Open Data DCAT Catalogue
- Brussels Statistical Sector Boundaries

## Methods

- Open-data discovery and metadata exploration
- Census data processing using Python and Pandas
- GIS analysis using GeoPandas
- Workforce composition analysis by citizenship, age, and region

## Preliminary Findings

- Non-Belgian citizens represented approximately 42% of construction workers employed in Brussels.
- EU citizens formed the largest migrant group within the construction workforce.
- Brussels displayed a substantially higher reliance on migrant construction labour than Flanders and Wallonia.
